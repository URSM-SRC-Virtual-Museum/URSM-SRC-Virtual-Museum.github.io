About the Congress

Cultivating Research Excellence

The URS Morong Student Research Congress 2026 is a campus-based competitive congress organized by the Office of the Campus Research and Development. It aims to cultivate research excellence and innovation among student researchers by providing a structured platform for undergraduate students to present and defend their completed research projects before an expert panel and the academic community.

36

Research Presentations

12 per category across three research paradigms

9

Expert Evaluators

3 qualified faculty judges per research category

100+

Expected Participants

Presenters, advisers, observers, and evaluators

Objectives

1

Provide a structured platform for presenting and defending completed research projects

2

Recognize and reward research excellence across descriptive, developmental, and experimental research

3

Develop competencies in oral presentation, scientific communication, and scholarly defense

4

Facilitate peer learning through diverse research topics and methodologies

5

Strengthen understanding of research quality standards and ethical conduct

6

Identify promising student researchers for advanced training and graduate studies

7

Create networking opportunities among students, faculty mentors, and evaluators

8

Document best practices and disseminate exemplary student research outputs

Research Categories

Each presentation is allocated 15 minutes: 10 minutes for presentation and 5 minutes for Q&A

Category 1 · EARTS

Descriptive Research

Systematically documents and describes phenomena, characteristics, and patterns through comprehensive data collection and analysis.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Research Problem & Objectives (15 pts)
  • Methodology & Design (25 pts)
  • Results & Analysis (25 pts)
  • Conclusions & Implications (15 pts)
  • Presentation & Communication (20 pts)

Participant Information

Submission Guidelines

Eligibility Criteria

  • Currently enrolled undergraduate students in good academic standing
  • Completed and defended research projects within the current or previous academic year
  • Research must have received approval from thesis/research advisers
  • Each research team may submit one entry per category
  • Research must be original work conducted by the student researcher(s)

Full-Paper Submission

  • Manuscript must follow IMRAD format (Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion)
  • Adhere to APA 7th edition formatting guidelines
  • Submit the completed Research Full-Paper Submission Form
  • Include 3–5 keywords describing the research
  • Incomplete or non-IMRAD manuscripts will be returned for revision
